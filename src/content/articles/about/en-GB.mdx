<ArticleBody>

Information on elections should be easy to find, and easy to use. In 2026, Malaysians should not have to rely on PDFs and scattered sources to deeply understand their own democracy. ElectionData.MY exists to fill that gap. It is the public face of the [Malaysian Election Corpus (MECo)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-025-06502-7): a civic and academic effort to digitise, clean, and open-source data on every Malaysian election from 1955 to the present day.

Every candidate, every party, every result—in a single site built to make Malaysian election data genuinely accessible.

## A standing offer to SPR

ElectionData.MY demonstrates that election data can be made open in a seamless and cost-efficient way. In an ideal world, SPR itself would already be performing this function, particularly given its access to the full ecosystem of election data.

However, institutional change takes time. In the spirit of nation-building, I therefore make an unconditional offer to SPR: either to help SPR build and maintain an official open data platform from the ground up, or to partner directly with SPR so that ElectionData.MY becomes its official open data provider. Either way, the offer is entirely pro bono. No strings attached, and no expiry.

The long-term hope is simple: that this platform becomes unnecessary—or at most, complementary to an official site.

Until then, the work continues.
