<ArticleBody>

Election information should be public, searchable, and easy to use. In 2026, Malaysians should not have to rely on scattered PDFs, inconsistent spreadsheets, or inaccessible archives to understand their own elections. ElectionData.MY exists to fill that gap. It is the public face of the [Malaysian Election Corpus (MECo)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-025-06502-7): a civic and academic effort to digitise, clean, and open-source data on every election held in Malaysia from 1955, the country's first general election, to the present day.

Every candidate, every party, every result—in a single site built to make Malaysian election data genuinely accessible.

## A standing offer to SPR

ElectionData.MY has demonstrated that comprehensive, high-quality election data can be made open in a seamless and cost-efficient way. In an ideal world, SPR itself would already be performing this function, particularly given its access to the full ecosystem of election data.

However, institutional change takes time. In the spirit of nation-building, I therefore make an unconditional and open-ended offer to SPR: either to help build and maintain an official open election data platform from the ground up, or to partner directly with SPR so that ElectionData.MY becomes its official open data provider. Either way, the offer is entirely pro bono. No strings attached, and no expiry for as long as I am alive.

The long-term hope is simple: that SPR eventually steps up, and this platform becomes unnecessary—or at most, complementary to an official public site.

My goal was never to get credit for this. It was to make sure it exists.
